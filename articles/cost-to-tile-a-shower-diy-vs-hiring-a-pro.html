Cost to Tile a Shower DIY vs Hiring a Pro

By DIY Renovation Expert | 2026-07-12 | Bathroom Renovation Costs
Share:

When tackling a bathroom renovation project, one of the key components is tiling a shower. Homeowners often face a dilemma deciding whether to DIY the tiling or hire a professional to get the job done. In this section, we will explore the costs associated with both options, highlighting the pros and cons of each approach.

Tiling a Shower DIY Costs

The cost of tiling a shower DIY can vary depending on the materials and tools required. Here's a breakdown of estimated costs:

  • Flooring Tiles: $3 to $10 per square foot, depending on the type and quality of tile.
  • Wall Tiles: $2 to $8 per square foot, depending on the type and quality of tile.
  • Tile Adhesive and Grout: $10 to $30 per 20-pound bag.
  • Cement Board and Backer Board: $10 to $30 per sheet.
  • Tools (Trowel, Level, Notched Tool): $50 to $100.

Assuming an average shower size of 40 square feet, the total cost for DIY materials would be approximately $500 to $1,500. Additionally, homeowners should also consider the cost of their time, which may range from a few days to a week, depending on their experience level.

Tiling a Shower Hiring a Pro Costs

Hiring a professional contractor to tile a shower can be more expensive, but it ensures a higher level of quality and reduces the risk of errors or accidents. Here are estimated costs associated with hiring a professional:

  • Contractor Labor: $2 to $5 per square foot.
  • Tile and Materials: $5 to $15 per square foot, depending on the type and quality of tile.
  • Licensing and Insurance Fees: $500 to $1,000.

For a 40-square-foot shower, the total cost of hiring a professional would be approximately $2,000 to $5,000. However, this cost includes the contractor's labor, materials, and any necessary permits or inspections.

Pros and Cons of Each Approach

DIV Tiling:

  • Cost-effective
  • Opportunity to learn new skills
  • Flexibility in design and layout
  • Can be time-consuming and physically demanding
  • May require additional materials and tools

Hiring a Pro:

  • Guaranteed quality and workmanship
  • Reduced risk of errors or accidents
  • Less time-consuming and physically demanding
  • Higher cost
  • Lack of control over design and layout

Conclusion

When deciding whether to DIY or hire a professional to tile a shower, homeowners should consider their level of experience, time constraints, and budget. While DIY tiling can save money and be a fun learning experience, hiring a professional ensures a high-quality result and reduces the risk of errors or accidents. It's essential to weigh the pros and cons of each approach before making an informed decision.
