Cost Comparison: Fiberglass vs Acrylic Showers

Fiberglass and acrylic showers are two popular options for bathroom renovations. Both materials offer a sleek and modern appearance, but they differ in terms of cost, durability, and installation requirements. In this section, we will compare the costs associated with installing fiberglass and acrylic showers to help you make an informed decision for your DIY project.

Fiberglass Showers

Fiberglass showers are a cost-effective option for bathroom renovations. The cost of a fiberglass shower can range from $500 to $3,000, depending on the size and features of the unit. Here are some factors to consider when estimating the cost of a fiberglass shower:

  • Size: Larger showers with multiple jets and seats will cost more than a standard-sized shower.
  • Features: Add-ons such as built-in benches, shelves, or lighting can increase the cost of the shower.
  • Installation: Hiring a professional to install the shower can add several hundred dollars to the overall cost.

Acrylic Showers

Acrylic showers are a popular choice for bathroom renovations due to their durability and ease of maintenance. The cost of an acrylic shower can range from $1,000 to $6,000, depending on the size, features, and quality of the unit. Here are some factors to consider when estimating the cost of an acrylic shower:

  • Size: While acrylic showers may appear similar to fiberglass showers, they can be more expensive due to their increased durability and scratch resistance.
  • Features: Custom features such as built-in seats, shelves, or lighting can significantly increase the cost of an acrylic shower.
  • Quality: High-end acrylic showers with advanced features and premium materials can cost upwards of $6,000.

Other Factors to Consider

When comparing the costs of fiberglass and acrylic showers, it's essential to consider other factors that may impact your overall budget. These include:

  • Waterproofing and sealing: Both fiberglass and acrylic showers require waterproofing and sealing to ensure they function correctly and last for a long time.
  • Plumbing and electrical: Any additional plumbing or electrical work required for the shower, such as installing a new water heater or adding lighting, can add to the overall cost.
  • Permits and inspections: Depending on your local regulations, you may need to obtain permits and undergo inspections to ensure your DIY shower installation meets safety standards.

In conclusion, the cost of a fiberglass shower can range from $500 to $3,000, while an acrylic shower can cost between $1,000 and $6,000. While both materials offer a sleek and modern appearance, acrylic showers are generally more durable and require less maintenance. However, the ultimate cost of your shower will depend on the size, features, and quality of the unit, as well as any additional factors that may impact your budget.
