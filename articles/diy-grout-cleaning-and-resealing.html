DIY Grout Cleaning and Resealing: A Step-by-Step Guide

Grout, the material used to fill the gaps between tiles, is prone to staining and discoloration, affecting the overall appearance of your bathroom. Cleaning and resealing the grout is a crucial part of bathroom maintenance, which we'll explore in this section.

Cleaning the Grout

  • Prepare a Solution:** Mix a solution of 1 cup of baking soda, 1 cup of white vinegar, and 1 tablespoon of mild dish soap.
  • Apply the Solution:** Apply the solution to the grout lines using an old toothbrush or a grout brush. Work in small sections to avoid over-saturating the grout.
  • Scrub the Grout:** Use a scrub brush to gently scrub the grout lines, working in a circular motion. Rinse the brush frequently to avoid re-depositing dirt.
  • Rinse the Grout:** Use a clean, damp cloth to wipe away the solution and dirt. Change the cloth frequently to avoid re-depositing dirt.
  • Neutralize the Solution:** Apply a solution of 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of white vinegar to the grout lines to neutralize any pH imbalances.
  • Dry the Grout:** Allow the grout to air dry completely before applying a sealer.

Cleaning Tough Stains

For tougher stains, you may need to use a more aggressive cleaning solution. Mix a solution of equal parts hydrogen peroxide and baking soda, and apply it to the stained grout. Let it sit for 30 minutes before scrubbing and rinsing as described above.

Resealing the Grout

  • Choose a Sealer:** Select a grout sealer that suits your needs. Acrylic and siliconate-based sealers are popular options.
  • Apply the Sealer:** Apply the sealer to the grout lines, working in small sections. Use a grout brush to apply the sealer evenly.
  • Allow the Sealer to Dry:** Follow the manufacturer's instructions for drying time, typically several minutes to an hour.
  • Apply Additional Coats:** For maximum protection, apply 2-3 coats of sealer, allowing each coat to dry before applying the next.
  • Seal the Grout Completely:** Once the sealer is fully dry, use a clean, damp cloth to wipe away any excess sealer.

Remember to always follow the manufacturer's instructions for any cleaning solutions or sealers used. Regular cleaning and sealing of the grout will help maintain its appearance and extend its lifespan.
